About Me

Hello! I am Yifu Zhang, a Master of Mathematics (MMath) student at the University of Oxford. I have consistently achieved first‑class honours throughout my undergraduate degree and was elected as the first‑year representative of the undergraduate committee. My academic journey began at Keystone Academy in Beijing where I completed the International Baccalaureate Diploma with a score of 43/45, earning a full Keystone Scholarship that covered the entire cost of my high school and first year of university. Beyond coursework, I am passionate about mathematical research, particularly in the areas of approximation theory and analytic number theory. I enjoy tackling problems at the intersection of theory and computation and continuously strive to deepen my understanding of mathematics.

Research Interests & Projects

Approximation Theory

In my recent project supervised by Prof. Yuji Nakatsukasa (Oct 2024 – present), I developed an algorithm for function approximation under heteroskedastic noise using Chebyshev interpolation. The algorithm improves both the time complexity and accuracy of existing approaches, and the results are being prepared for a preprint and journal submission in July 2025.

Analytic Number Theory

During the summer of 2023, under the guidance of Dr. Ofir Gorodetsky, I investigated the “anatomy of integers”. In this project I extended a lower bound in a paper by Eberhard, Green and Ford (2016) concerning permutations that fix sets of certain lengths. This work deepened my appreciation for both the combinatorial and analytic facets of number theory.

My broader research interests span approximation theory, numerical analysis, analytic number theory and mathematical problems where theoretical insights inform efficient computation. I am keen to continue exploring new ideas in these fields and to collaborate with researchers who share similar passions.
