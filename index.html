Introduction

I am a Master of Mathematics student (MMath) at the University of Oxford, interested in numerical analysis, approximation theory and numerical linear algebra. Recent work includes algorithms for function approximation under heteroskedastic noise using Chebyshev interpolation for noisy function approximation.

Biography

I finished my first three years at Oxford with Triple Firsts, and I am currently looking for PhD opportunities. Before that I was a student at Keystone Academy, Beijing, where I graduated with the International Baccalaureate Diploma (43/45), and was awarded the Keystone Scholarship.

Research Interests

  • Function approximation under noisy sampling (heteroskedastic & dependent noise)
  • Numerical linear algebra and randomized algorithms

Selected Projects

Efficient Function Approximation under Heteroskedastic Noise

Extends Chebyshev interpolation with noise-aware sampling to improve uniform error and runtime in heteroskedastic settings.

  • Area: Approximation / Numerical LA
  • Status: preprint & journal submission
  • Supervision: Prof. Yuji Nakatsukasa (Oxford)

Analytic Number Theory — Permutations & Fixed Sets

Investigated bounds related to permutations fixing sets of certain lengths, building on Eberhard–Ford–Green (2016).

  • Area: Probabilistic/Combinatorial number theory
  • Supervision: Dr. Ofir Gorodetsky
